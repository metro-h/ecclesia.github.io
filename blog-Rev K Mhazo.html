When Love Reigns

Rev K Mhazo, Harare Zimbabwe October 2022
Comments 59K Views
Introduction

John 3:16, “For God so loved the world that He gave His only begotten Son, that whoever believes in Him should not perish but have everlasting life. For God did not send His Son into the world to condemn the world, but that the world through Him might be saved”. We are now in the festive season during which we celebrate the love that God extended towards us by giving His only son Jesus Christ to redeem us from the bondage of sin and death. The love of God is not limited to a certain race, class or nationality, tribe or social status. It is open and accessible to everyone who can believe in Jesus Christ.

We all deserved to be condemned because of sin. It would have been right for God to have pronounced judgment upon the whole human race, but behold, He graciously considered to forgive us. The sentence of condemnation was therefore deferred for those who have not yet believed in Christ. But, although Jesus did not come then to condemn the world, the time is coming when he will return to judge the living and the dead. 2 Corinthians 5:10 says that “for we must all appear before the judgment seat of Christ, that each one may receive the things done in the body, according to what he has done, whether good or bad”. If we do not accept the free gift of Jesus Christ that God gave us, we will be condemned. As Romans 5:8 says, “…God demonstrates His own love toward us, in that while we were still sinners, Christ died for us.” While God is holy, and there is no sin in him, He loved sinful and undeserving people like you and me. His desire is that all mankind may be saved (1 Timothy 2:3–4). Such is the great love of God as 1 John 3:1–3 expresses, “Behold what manner of love the Father has bestowed on us, that we should be called the children of God. Therefore the world does not know us, because it did not know Him. Beloved, now we are children of God; and it has not yet been revealed what we shall be, but we know that when He is revealed, we shall be like Him, for we shall see Him as He is. And everyone who has this hope in Him purifies himself, just as He is pure.” Volumes of literature can be written about the love of God for mankind without exhausting its extraordinary depth and richness. 1 John 3 calls us to behold, contemplate and marvel upon the great love of God.

But the world in which we live has forgotten how to hear God's voice. We are now more accustomed to paying attention to the many voices that surround us every day. Sadly, only a small portion of these voices guide us down the route that God has predetermined for us. Is it feasible for us to teach our kids to talk and act with respect and attention towards their parents, grandparents, and other people in authority in a culture that encourages individuality, contempt, and insolence? Yes, it is the answer. We live in this world, yet we are not of it, as the Bible teaches. The underlying principles revealed in the Word of God enable parents to establish a family atmosphere infused with a spiritual standard of living. Children need to be taught to respect people in positions of authority. The success of your child in the future will change as a result of this lesson, which is imparted via both word and deed. You must uphold honour if you demand it. The only command in the Bible that confers long life and blessings on your descendants is to honor your parents. "Children, obey your parents in the Lord; it is right to do so. According to Ephesians 6:1-3, "Honor your parents, for this is the first and great commandment, with a promise that it may go well with you and that you may live a long life on the earth. “Thus says the Lord of hosts, the God of Israel: Because you have obeyed the command of Jonadab your father and have kept all his precepts and have done according to all that he commanded you, therefore thus says the Lord of hosts, the God of Israel: Jonadab son of Rechab shall never fail [to have] a man [descendant] to stand before Me” (Jeremiah 35:18-19).

Overall, the length, breadth, depth and the height of God’s love goes beyond human comprehension. This reminds me of a song we used to sing during our children’s church days back then about the love of God; “The love of Jesus is so wonderful x3. Wonderful love! So high you can’t go over it! So wide you can’t go round it! So low you can’t get under it! Wonderful love!” Beloved, we did not deserve such great love. We neither could claim nor merit to be called the sons of God. We were the children of the wicked one, from the fall of Adam in the Garden of Eden. Since then the devil captured the human race up to the death of Jesus Christ on the cross of Calvary. Therefore, it is necessary for us to accept and live with such transforming love that moved us from misery to greatness as we are now co–heirs with Christ. When he appears in his second coming, we shall be glorious like him as John 17:24 states, “…Father, I will that they also whom thou hast given me be with me where I am, that they may behold my glory.” Thus, we should abstain from all forms of evil and live in love according to our new narrative in Chris; 1 John 4:7–8, “beloved, let us love one another, for love is of God; and everyone who loves is born of God, and knows God. He who doesn't love doesn't know God, for God is love

While unbelievers may be selective, the Bible teaches believers to love unconditionally (including our enemies – Mathew 5:43) as God does. Apostle Paul explained to the Corinthians that “love is patient and is kind; love doesn't envy. Love doesn't brag, is not proud, doesn't behave itself inappropriately, doesn't seek its own way, [love is not selfish} is not provoked, takes no account of evil; doesn't rejoice in unrighteousness, but rejoices with the truth; bears all things, believes all things, hopes all things, endures all things.” (1 Corinthians 13:4–7) Imagine if our homes, churches, industries, governments, etcetera were led by loving people! I have no doubt that this world could be a better place to live, free from wars and poverty

Conclusion

When love reigns, people exercise patience and kindness with each other. When love reigns, envy, pride, selfishness and evil end! Love promotes forgiveness and empathy. As we enjoy this festive season, let us reflect on love and live it for the best of our personal, family, denominational, organizational, national and global lives. God help us to allow love to reign in our lives in the name of Jesus Christ! Amen!
