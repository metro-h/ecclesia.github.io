Opportunities And Challenges of Campus Ministry

Pastor Zodwa Mutisi, Bulawayo October 2022
Comments 33k Views
Introduction

Campus ministry is so important for the continual engagement of the gospel during learning seasons at schools. It is strategic beyond sharing the gospel, towards grooming future church and marketplace leaders. If we do not engage students at school, many of them lose direction and may eventually succumb to devilish temptations and enslavement. Thus, the church should be tactically positioned with relevant programs to continue providing spiritual guidance at campus. Over the years, most Christian students who were actively engaged in ecclesial activities on campus grew and became effective ecclesiological and marketplace gospel ministers. It should be therefore premeditated for any serious church to facilitate programs that consistently and effectively engage students. In order for us to appreciate and redouble efforts to accomplish this ministry, let us look at some challenges that are usually encountered in running a successful campus ministry.

Opportunities of Campus Ministry

  • Transformational ministry Opportunities at campus

    Meanwhile, there are also opportunities that we should remember, in campus ministry. Ministering the gospel with students is one of the best training grounds for gospel envoys. It is through sharing the gospel with young people that various gifts can be nurtured, upcoming leaders can be mentored and transformation agents can be grounded. Considering that universities and colleges gather young people from different backgrounds, impacting them with the gospel also prepares cross cultural marketplace missionaries and ecclesial ambassadors.

    1. Lack of Motivation

      Many times some churches failed to realize meaningful growth and impact on campus because of lack of motivation among the young people. It tales effective leadership to motivate students to take up the gospel regardless of any discouragements. Churches should therefore provide, raise, train, mentor and capacitate young people to serve God from their schooling times. Whenever ecclesial inspiration and support lacks, the majority of students tire and lose out. Financial resources Campus ministry is made up mainly of the non-working classes (students). We cannot run away from the fact that to effective campus ministry demand financial resources. Denominations that have strong local and city churches close to the campus have been very successful in running campus ministries. Usually, mother churches bear the burden of financial liabilities and sustain all the campus programs, for example, by providing Public (PA) systems, funding team building activities, and exchange initiatives. Without economic sustainability, most churches struggle to run campus ministries.

    2. Institutional policies

      Institutions of higher learning have different policies that guide their operations. Common institutional provision in Zimbabwe is the chaplaincy division which oversee all religious activities in any university or college. However, not all colleges and universities have space for ecclesiastic activities as there are infrastructural challenges. Some universities do not allow Church services to be held in the campus premises. So, progressive churches usually look for any venues close to the campus to continue engaging with students. Many churches fail to sustain alternatives without financial challenges and eventually neglect students’ ministry.

    Conclusion

    Additionally, campus ministry retains professionals to the mainstream churches. If we do not follow and keep up with our church members at school, other religions will eventually take them! Yet, spiritually, socially, economically and politically stable churches are built up by professionals that they raised throughout their studying journeys. It is highly commendable for us to observe challenges, and opportunities of campus ministry to do all that we can towards reviving, establishing and sustaining campus ministry for the best of the mission of God, our children and churches.
