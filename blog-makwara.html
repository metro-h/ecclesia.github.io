Prepare for retirement: A pastoral reflection

Pastor Blessing Makwara Harare, Zimbabwe October 2023
Comments 39K Views

Introduction

Simply put, to retire is leaving one's job and stopping to work. This can be by free choice or forced by the law and other circumstances. Some employers stipulate that one must retire when they reach age of 65, some 60 and others 55 for early retirement. If you enter the labour market, prepare to exit as well. One day you will retire, either through legislation limits, age, health challenges, disability, and retrenchment among other causes. One reality many are trying to disrupt and deny is that every living organism that is born will grow, wax strong, achieve peak performance then commences reversal, become flux, slow and die. Remember when you woke up yesterday, you felt alive, energetic, went about with your day's chores or tasks but there came a time the mind and body began to feel weak and less agile, till you slowed everything and went to bed. You were tired. That is normal.

In life such cycle seems to be a fact. The question is; are you ready to retire or be retired? One of my mentors told me that young man as you step into this office immediately begin to plan leaving the same. This was true wisdom! Ever thought why people everywhere agree that there is a certain age and stage in life that one must work and then stop. They talk of efficiencies at the workplace. A debate rages even today about whether a pastor can say I am now ‘tired’ so am stepping aside to focus on tasks that a tired person can only do. Others see it unimaginable that a pastor can be forced to retire. This is especially said by those who believe only in full-time Ministry, giving up the vocational to only serve the Lord - to avoid serving two masters, God and Mammon.

Our reality

I think our theologies contribute much to our state of affairs today. This is about our views on money, wealth, work, rest and the world generally which knowingly or unknowingly influences our choices and actions in life. Many times we have heard reports of pastors collapsing at the pulpit, or on their way down from the pulpit, or right in front of everyone. Today we have cases of pastors who think it is evil to listen to their bodies, abuse the flesh till the flesh gives up on them – forcing retirement. A few statistics indicate pastors committing suicide while mental health is fast becoming a thorn in the flesh for church workers. Some don't even believe in conventional medicine and only believe prayer alone is what God has ordained as means to ensure health. Sadly, the reality is that many suffer silently till death and the slogan "nguva yaMwari yanga yakwana" (God’s time had come) is echoed everywhere yet scripture talks of full life beyond 70 years. With poor job security in Zimbabwe, high formal unemployment and underemployment in the informal sector, it is not guaranteed that you will still earn that pay, salary or allowance from church as the sheep are not producing any milk. Since the days of COVID-19 many churches have struggled to raise money through tithes and offerings. Chenjerai kukama mombe kusvika kwakungobuda ropa chete (Be careful to avoid milking until only blood oozes). Here you here some pastors citing the widow of Zarephath case and not the widow of one of the prophets case.

Unique features of the work: Leadership style

The church leadership style is consultative and office bearers are so humble. Their humility is the hallmark of the church’s growth. Leaders respect senior pastors through various ways such as tasking them to introduce speakers at regional, national events as well as closing events with prayers. The profiling of ministry is something that one cannot fail to notice. Nobody goes to the stage unless they have specific tasks to perform. That way, one can hardly notice who the leaders, i.e. chairman of the Regional council is or even who the members of the team. Currently, Pastor Mamba is the regional chairman. He sometimes leads by absence. When anyone thinks he will go up to say something he delegates or is strategically not at that point. Such aspects seem to advance missionary servant-hood!

Today, there are no more cities of the Priests and Levites except monastries run by a few mainline churches. Yes Paul had a right like we do (servants of the altar), yet this was not absolute. In fact he argues that he didn't claim lest some may be offended with the money thing in church, as many surely do today. Money as it is needed to answer all things, has become a stumbling block for the poor and vulnerable. We have pastors today who refuse to be assigned to a new local church outside the cities and towns. It's because apart from earning through tithe and offering, most don't want to use other skills and talents. Some hold on to the teaching that store your treasure in spiritual terms and not on earthly things yet the main emphasis of that teaching seem ignored. Church administration systems must look at this area and empower pastors to prepare for retirement, but much more this period before one turns 55 or 65.

Invest in your health

Don't just think of income, think about your body, your health and act accordingly. Our Zimbabwean musical hero Oliver Mutukudzi (aka Tuku) sang the track "mupfumi ndiyani" (Who is rich?) messaging that mupfumi ndiye uyo ane hutano (the rich is one who is healthy). As our bodies are the temple of the Holy Spirit, we must pay attention to live healthy lifestyles watching how and what we eat as well as having frequent bodily exercises, this Apostle Paul affirms as beneficial/profitable. Let us not just exercise our tongue and jaws while eating but as well our muscles and flex the joints too. You may still be fervent in Spirit but can't do much when your body is frail! Even if we give you one more mountain, you may not even be able to clear the land yourself. Maybe for a legacy project, yes. Pastor, find time to walk around, jog, do the press-ups, or skip the rope. It's part of investing in retirement. I have also learnt to say NO to some invitations to minister or serve, especially when my body tells me so. Life expectancy is Zimbabwe is said to be under 40 years, think about it. There is a time for everything! Think about this reality. As we think so we become. Instead of being shocked with the thought of a time when you can't work to earn, instead, embrace it and plan for it.

Real story

There was a time in my working episode that I used to get airtime and fuel allowance every week, which oftentimes I didn't exhaust before the next signing. I could call anyone anytime, and travel anywhere without stress. I got used to this and adjusted my lifestyle accordingly. Ever heard that there is never a time money is enough? But a time came when all this was gone, you can imagine the discomfort, anxiety and frustration. I used to drive different vehicles associated with wealthy persons and yet my personal car was a Honda Fit. It came a time that those nice off-roads had to be taken away, it was Honda Fit's time. Thank goodness I had the Honda Fit, imagine if I didn't own this small car. Back to public transport – kombi, mushikashika and all right? My point is think, plan and act regarding how life will be when you are no longer doing that job or getting that monthly pay.

Invest in diverse income sources

Even when one has been formally employed and paying pension contributions, the volatile economy will humble you. The monthly pay-out today is how much compared to cost of living monthly? You cannot bank on this. Worse, most churches don't follow labour laws and most pastors don't have pension even. Saka uchaita sei? (So, what shall you do?)You need another income avenue. While you still can earn it, think of the dry season ahead (retirement).

Real Story

Like Joseph (the second in command in Egypt at one point the greatest civilisations), during the days of good earning, I realised my contract will not be renewed even though I loved my job. I recalled my passion and interests that were elsewhere, in farming. So my wife and I managed to secure a small piece of land, fenced it, build a decent cottage, drilled a borehole, constructed house for broilers and a shed-net greenhouse from savings. It was not a roller-coaster. First drilling produced a dry hole, more than a thousand United States dollars lost in a moment. Still, we drilled again after a while. Sometime later, we thought of introducing piggery, built the pigsty and later secured breeding stock. This definitely was not a short-term project, we were preparing for the long haul. I invested in knowledge and became hands-on while still formally employed. When it was time to go, at least the projects could self sustain. We had assets that could be monetized to cover the years of unemployment. For me actually it was about seventeen months jobless, no income coming.

Invest in a saving culture

Saving money and not enjoying it through consumption is not easy but if you have a goal, you endure all the self-denials of leisure and shopping and traveling. No matter how small your income currently is a pastor, learn to save money. Those gifts, “sown seeds” and allowances should be saved. Saving is not keeping the remainder but making an intentional act of removing and saving a certain amount. These days it’s not best to put money under the pillow, consider an asset to buy and store value. Please buying a car is not saving, most cars depreciate value fast. As part of saving, learn to live within your means, the remaining income. Don’t compare yourself with another pastor or family

Invest in your spouse

I am so grateful that my spouse is not a lazy person but very intelligent and diligent. Young people, when thinking of marriage, calculate well and ensure your loved one is not lazy. Haisingori yemafeatures and feelings nyaya iyi (It’s not about body features and beauty). Two are better than one in many respects. Some pastors don’t allow their wives to do any work or selling, hanzi zvinodzikisira hufundisi hwavo, shuwa here? (They say it belittles their pastoral status. Is it so?) Today the tide is no longer easy for man to be sole breadwinners, those days are long gone. Invest in your spouse, let them learn and earn. We need the Priscila and Aquila of today. Like Apostle Paul, he provided for his needs and those around him from his tent-making project/business/skill so that he never became spiteful for those churches who didn't support him materially/financially. Even if you are a pastor, as gifted as you are, you don't have daytime alltime. Imagine a pastor being a farmer, a marketer, an entrepreneur as well as a community resource? Surely you won't starve. Your family won't curse your G-d or Ministry. I know in my church movement, many pastors are now overdue retirement as per labour laws in the country but it's a sad state to see very few who own any property, or have a thriving income generation venture or with social capital that can catapult them from suffering. Life expectancy is Zimbabwe is said to be under 40 years, think about it.Even my favourite poet spoke about the age of 25 as normal stage in life for one to live independently and sustainably, yet alas it is not so anymore. He still has dreams, so he encourages us all to never give up. Be shrewd with your time, talent and talents.

Conclusion

Human beings are designed to function effectively during the day while most animals thrive at night. The Lord knew this wisdom, that night will soon come, so He had to do most if not all of His assignment before He was arrested and killed in Jerusalem (night time came) "I must work while it is still day...". As the famous sons of Issachar, everyone needs this gift and wisdom to know the times and what one must do. Indeed, it is about knowing the existence of evil days so you place yourself to redeem the time. You may be young but that doesn't last forever. You may be old but there is yet another level of older till being the oldest. If you have been insulted by this piece, am sorry, it wasn’t the aim. If you found this challenging, you are the right person to have read this piece!
