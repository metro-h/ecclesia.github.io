Going beyond Prayers: Strategic Personal Planning for the year

Rev Dr.Ct Neuso, Harare October 2023
Comment 55K Views
Introduction

Towards the end and beginning of every year, a significant number of believers come up with written and unwritten resolutions in preparation for the New Year. Few of these resolutions come to fruition. The major reason of failure is lack of personal strategic planning. A strategy is a plan of action designed to achieve a longterm or overall aim. Every resolution should be backed up by a strategy. Personal strategic planning is all about rightly placing yourself for success.

Failing to plan is planning to fail. Faith without personal strategic planning is a recipe for failure and frustration. God is a planner (the story of creation aptly sums it up all). Likewise, believers should also plan strategically. Jesus Christ also worked strategically. Here are two good examples of how he valued planning and being orderly. He said to his disciples, “have them sit down in groups of about fifty each” (Luke 9:14). Luke later on recorded Jesus teaching, “Suppose one of you wants to build a tower. Will he not first sit down and estimate the cost to see if he has enough money to complete it” (Luke 14:28). If God the Father and Jesus the Son could operate strategically, you and I should do likewise. In the Bible, the success of Joshua is demonstrated by how he strategically planned for the fall of Jericho and for the battles he won resoundingly. Nehemiah didn’t just woke up and started rebuilding the fallen walls. He assessed the situation first then mobilized the resources and started working. David needed men who could think and plan strategically, and God gave him the sons of Issachar (1 Chronicles 12:32). Paul, in the book of Acts, and in his epistles showed that he worked with strategic plans that were governed by the Holy Spirit. This is just to mention few notable figures in the Bible who backed their work with personal strategic planning. Here are some of the basic principles of strategic planning which we should understand and apply when it comes to making resolutions: have a mission, have a vision, formulate objectives, carry out SWOT analysis, have core values, carry out a feasibility study, and have action plans. These principles should also apply to personal strategic planning. Many times believers come up with resolutions at the beginning of a year accompanied by prayer and fasting but without personal strategic planning hence the failure of most of the resolutions.

But the world in which we live has forgotten how to hear God's voice. We are now more accustomed to paying attention to the many voices that surround us every day. Sadly, only a small portion of these voices guide us down the route that God has predetermined for us. Is it feasible for us to teach our kids to talk and act with respect and attention towards their parents, grandparents, and other people in authority in a culture that encourages individuality, contempt, and insolence? Yes, it is the answer. We live in this world, yet we are not of it, as the Bible teaches. The underlying principles revealed in the Word of God enable parents to establish a family atmosphere infused with a spiritual standard of living. Children need to be taught to respect people in positions of authority. The success of your child in the future will change as a result of this lesson, which is imparted via both word and deed. You must uphold honour if you demand it. The only command in the Bible that confers long life and blessings on your descendants is to honor your parents. "Children, obey your parents in the Lord; it is right to do so. According to Ephesians 6:1-3, "Honor your parents, for this is the first and great commandment, with a promise that it may go well with you and that you may live a long life on the earth. “Thus says the Lord of hosts, the God of Israel: Because you have obeyed the command of Jonadab your father and have kept all his precepts and have done according to all that he commanded you, therefore thus says the Lord of hosts, the God of Israel: Jonadab son of Rechab shall never fail [to have] a man [descendant] to stand before Me” (Jeremiah 35:18-19).

As an individual do you have a personal mission? That is the reason or purpose for your existence. Do you know why God brought you on earth? Are your resolutions in line with your personal mission on earth? Are you making your resolutions according to your personal vision? That is your dream intent – where you want to be in the future. Have you formulated objectives which indicate what is critical to yourself? In other words, objectives are the goals or resolutions you are trying to achieve in the following year or so. These objectives should be SMART. Are they Specific? Do you really know what you want to achieve in the following year? Are your objectives or resolutions Measurable? Are the objectives or resolutions Achievable? Do not formulate extremely difficult resolutions though they should be challenging and motivating to pursue them. Objectives should be Realistic. Do not come up with ridiculous resolutions. Objectives should be Timeous or Time bound. When will they be attained or achieved? You need also to analyze the environment using SWOT (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats) analysis tool in which these resolutions will be applied. Find out the areas you are Strong at and maximize your efforts and resources there, make sure that your resolutions have a strong bias in those areas and minimize them where you are Weak, the good thing is that you have got control over yourself (internal environment). You have got no control over the market or task environment, and macro environment (external environment) but you can maximize on the Opportunities that can be brought about by these other two environments but minimize aligning your resolutions in areas where there are Threats. Do you have the core values which are the fundamental beliefs of yourself? These guiding principles (core values) dictate behavior and can help you understand the difference between right and wrong or otherwise navigate the difficult challenges that you may face as you try to fulfill your resolutions. Do you have the character to pursue your objectives? Carry out also a feasibility study. That is an assessment of whether your resolutions will succeed by analyzing your core competencies (self analysis), the market or task environment, and macro environment. Feasibility study has got an impact on the achievement of the resolutions.

Conclusion

Finally you have to come up with action plans. Just having objectives or resolutions without actions is what the Bible equate to Faith without action which is dead (James 2:26). An action plan lists what steps must be taken to achieve an objective or resolution. It breaks down the resolution into actionable steps that can be easily followed and tracked. The purpose of an action plan is to clarify what resources are required to achieve the objective and formulate a timeline for when specific tasks need to be completed. These actions needs to be monitored, evaluated, and where possible adjusted accordingly because the external environment is dynamic and volatile. An effective leader (believer) execute the strategy and follows up on it. Personal strategic planning should be carried in partnership with God, for Bible says, “Commit to the Lord whatever you do, and he will establish your plans” (Proverbs 16:3). Further on the Bible mentioned that, “In their hearts humans plan their course, but the Lord establishes their steps” (Proverbs 16:9). It is my earnest prayer that as the year comes to an end as believers we should upload and exercise the strategic planning principles mentioned above, being lead and directed by the Holy Spirit under the Lordship of Jesus for we are more than conquerors in Jesus Christ. May the following two verses strengthen and give you hope as you plan for the New Year – “Being confident of this, that he who began a good work in you will carry it on to completion until the day of Christ Jesus” (Philippians 1:6); “The one who calls you is faithful, and he will do it” (1 Thessalonians 5:24).
