Love Others as you Love yourself

Simplisio Mukondo, Gweru Zimbabwe October 2022
Comments 25K Views
Introduction

Peace be with you! Well, it’s my pleasure to welcome you, ladies and gentlemen to this article with which I deliberate much on the four-lettered word called LOVE. This word looks small alphabetically, and might sound common to many, taking into cognizance the fact that almost everybody knows it; but it carries deeper meanings that influence human nature right across the orb. It is so challenging and demanding that if you take it for granted, you might simply be caught off guard. In fact, the word calls for total concentration from everyone involved because it is practical. Hence, the reason why I thought to talk about it in greater detail. Biblically, love has four got four types with Greek names which are: Agape, Eros, Philia and Storge. The Agape kind of love is unconditional/sacrificial. It is a major term in the Christian Bible, which is why it is often defined as “Christian Love”. This is love between God and His earthily people, and vice versa. John 3 v 16 explicitly shows this kind of love when God so loved the world that He gave His only begotten Son that whosoever believes in Him shall not perish but have eternal life. Eros love is romantic, i.e. between a husband and wife or boy and his beloved girlfriend. It involves passion and/or lust. Philia is the brotherly love—that kind of love which obliges you to love one another as you love yourself. It calls for friendship. Strong friends feel for each other. Storge is familial love whereby family members have passion for one another.

Form the above definitions, Philia and Storge look very similar. They bear almost the same meaning. It is all about loving one another as you love yourself, and that is what I am talking about. Their definitions come back to the above topic hence the need to scrutinize them closely. Dear brothers and sisters, what I really know is that most of us claim to be Godfearing and Christ followers. Of course, there’s nothing sinister about that. But the striking factor is, are we genuine God-fearing churchgoers or pretenders? Are we real Christ followers or we assume to be? These are multi-million questions which require deeper contemplation. If we are fervent stalwarts of our Lord Jesus Christ, let’s live by example. Do it in style. Be like Christ. The Lord, Himself, clearly put it across on Matthew 22 verse 39 that we should love one another as we do to ourselves. He said, referring to love, and I quote: “And the second is like unto it, you shall love your neighbor as yourself.”

If you prudently read through the first three books of the New Testament—Matthew, Mark and Luke—commonly referred to as the synoptic gospels—you will realize that Jesus’ ninety-five percent of the message was centered on brotherly love. He concentrated much on this type of love, trying to cultivate in human mind a sense of selflessness, togetherness, benevolence and consideration for others. He, in deed, highlighted that one cannot love God whom he is not seeing if he fails to love his own neighbor. If you love your neighbor, you won’t do anything bad against them at all, no matter what. True love seeks not her own and thinks no evil, as read with verse 5 of First Corinthians Chapter 13. The question is, are we correctly interpreting the word love or we just go through it in the Bible like any other? It boggles the mind to see how people treat each other in various facets of life every day. You wonder if this is done by God-fearing people or not. There’s so much practice of injustice, corruption, maltreatment, racism, and tribalism. Be it in the public or private sector, people are engaging in abhorrent practices which leave a lot to be desired. Nowadays it’s the money which does the talking! If you don’t have a fat wallet my friend, then forget about getting deserved help.

For instance, to get your child’s place at a better school, you have to bribe the authorities first. For your application letter to be accepted at a job-offering company, you have to meet the manager’s demands, either in cash or kind. To be promoted at your work place, it’s all based on who you know not what you know. No wonder why we are having so many substandard products and poor customer service in the market, it’s solely because of corruption. Because of selfishness, jealous and hatred, so many families are divided. The word love is now history to them. Churches are splitting every day because of greedy and inconsiderate leaders who value money more than the congregants whose spirits they are supposed to guide. They engage in nasty fights before the courts only to seize total control of the church and its proceedings. What a shame! In the social scene, bloody sisters are not seeing face to face only because they clashed over a potential suitor. Brothers from the same womb are now wild antagonists, hating each other like they are in a war-torn situation, only because they suspect each other of witchcraft.

Conclusion

Where are we going as the people of God? What are we really doing? The Bible tells us to seek peace with everybody, for without which nobody shall see God. How can you see God if you fail to support the needy? How shall you inherit the Kingdom of Heaven if you hate your sister like hell? How can you justify your status as the disciple of Jesus Christ if you fail to support your parents who are languishing in abject poverty? How can you stand your ground if you turn a blind eye to those who look up to you as their messiah? My dear, let’s practice love because love binds it all. Lastly, as we are approaching the Festive season, let’s embrace the spirit of giving. The Bible says blessed is the hand that gives. Let’s prepare to extend the generous hands. What you think is very small and valueless to you might be very useful to others. Remember there are so many orphans, widows and vulnerable people out there who expect a little from you for their morale boost. Just give them what you have. They will surely remember you for that in future. Let’s learn to love
