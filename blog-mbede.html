Keeping Low to stay High: The Power of Humility

Pastor Mbede, Bulawayo, Zimbabwe October 2022
Comments 21K Views
Introduction

Almost all of us pray that God may change our circumstances, but only few people pray for God to change their character. When some of us open our mouths to talk, lies just flow and we lie boldly. This is a character disorder. Many of us need to pray for transformation. Jacob wrestled with an angel. The angel touched his thigh and broke his long leg which he was using for mischief. One of the reasons why God cannot use many of us is that there are so many pressure points in our lives which He wants us to sort out. Unfortunately several fellows are not fixing their issues. God works on people point by point, and character by character.

There is no amount of riches you can have that can atone for character poverty. Money does not atone for lack of good character. If you want to know the kind of character that people have, watch them when they are quarrelling. When you hear what they are saying you will be baffled because they go to church and call themselves children of God. They have bad character, and such people can’t be humble unless the grace of God reaches them. When l came from Malawi in 1981, l was in a seminary training to be a Roman Catholic father. Few years later, l got served and trained myself to enjoy the word of God. I encountered scriptures that challenge my life on how to keep low and stay high;

Philippians 2:5-11, “The attitude you should have is the one that Christ Jesus had: He always had the nature of God, but he did not think that by force he should try to remain equal with God. Instead of this, of his own free will he gave up all he had, and took the nature of a servant. He became like a human being and appeared in human likeness. He was humble and walked the path of obedience all the way to death —his death on the cross. For this reason God raised him to the highest place above and gave him the name that is greater than any other name. And so, in honour of the name of Jesus all beings in heaven, on earth, and in the world below will fall on their knees, and all will openly proclaim that Jesus Christ is Lord, to the glory of God the Father”

Apostle Paul was one of the people who lived a life of humility. Philippians 1:21 adds that “For to me, to live is Christ and to die is gain”. Paul was arrested as he was jotting this letter because of his love for Jesus Christ, and he was not discouraged by such predicaments. Even when he faced death, he did not despair. His secrete was fully submitting himself to God. Thus, Paul lived for Christ, sacrificially. He did not base his life on circumstances, but on the eternal truth of the gospel.

Conclusion

If our purpose in life is to love Christ and serve him, God will give us joy even when we face danger and trouble. We will glorify him always. Every humble person is a person of Character; James 4:10, “Humble yourselves before the Lord, and he will lift you up”. God walks with the humble. Humility begets God’s promotion. Without humility, we may fast, pray and seek God in vain. Thought–provokingly, what we are in the sight of God is what we truly are. We cannot borrow humility. That is why the status of a nation lies in the character of its citizens. Humility is more valuable than even gold. Ugliness with good character is better than beauty with a bad character. Pride can catch up with us and eventually disgrace us. So, we need more of humble people in this world. God Himself is searching for men and women whose hearts are after Him. Such are those he seeks to use them
