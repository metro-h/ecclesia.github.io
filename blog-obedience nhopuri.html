Better than grave flowers: Honouring our parents today

Obedience Nopuri, HANGZHOU, China October 2023
Comments 20K Views
Introduction

The definition of honour in Greek is ‘to revere, prize, and value’. Honour is demonstrating respect for both merit and rank. This honour is not contingent on your parents' actions towards you or even on whether they were excellent parents. Instead, they deserve respect for who they are. The respect that children have for their parents nowadays does not need to change once the meaning of the word honour is understood. This commandment has, however, been interpreted differently for some people as a result of contemporary societal changes.

We may better understand the appropriate ways to honor parents if we first understand how not to do so. However, on Mount Sinai thousands of years ago, the Lord established His own commandment honouring our parents. In order for your days to be lengthy and for everything to go well for you, Jehovah your God instructed that you honour your parents (Deuteronomy 5:16). The respect that children should have for their parents is highly valued by God. This importance is clear from the fact that He included this advice in the Ten Commandments (Exodus 20:12). Because it is so significant to our Father, He opted to include it in the text. Obeying the Lord is rewarded. The Lord outlines the numerous blessings He will bestow upon His children in Deuteronomy 28, one of my favourite passages of Scripture, when we obey His voice and His commands. “If you fully obey the Lord your God and carefully follow all his commands I give you today, the Lord your God will set you high above all the nations on earth. All these blessings will come on you and accompany you if you obey the Lord your God (1-2)”. Considering that, when we pay attention to God's voice and carry out His commands, He will bless us. The abundance of joy, good health, divine favor, and provision are just a few of these blessings. What incredible promises, and all we have to do is obey and listen to His voice!

But the world in which we live has forgotten how to hear God's voice. We are now more accustomed to paying attention to the many voices that surround us every day. Sadly, only a small portion of these voices guide us down the route that God has predetermined for us. Is it feasible for us to teach our kids to talk and act with respect and attention towards their parents, grandparents, and other people in authority in a culture that encourages individuality, contempt, and insolence? Yes, it is the answer. We live in this world, yet we are not of it, as the Bible teaches. The underlying principles revealed in the Word of God enable parents to establish a family atmosphere infused with a spiritual standard of living. Children need to be taught to respect people in positions of authority. The success of your child in the future will change as a result of this lesson, which is imparted via both word and deed. You must uphold honour if you demand it. The only command in the Bible that confers long life and blessings on your descendants is to honor your parents. "Children, obey your parents in the Lord; it is right to do so. According to Ephesians 6:1-3, "Honor your parents, for this is the first and great commandment, with a promise that it may go well with you and that you may live a long life on the earth. “Thus says the Lord of hosts, the God of Israel: Because you have obeyed the command of Jonadab your father and have kept all his precepts and have done according to all that he commanded you, therefore thus says the Lord of hosts, the God of Israel: Jonadab son of Rechab shall never fail [to have] a man [descendant] to stand before Me” (Jeremiah 35:18-19).

The wisest man King Solomon, exhorted kids to respect their parents (Proverbs 1:8, 13:1, 30:17). We never outgrow God's command to honor our parents, even if we are no longer directly subject to their authority. Even Jesus, God the Son, deferred to both His heavenly Father (Matthew 26:39) and His earthly parents (Luke 2:51). We should strive to honor our parents in a way that glorifies God, following Christ's example. In both our actions and attitudes, we should respect our parents (Mark 7:6). Through our deeds, words, and actions, we seek to please and glorify God. God rewards us with a promise when we pay attention to and follow His commands. You should therefore pay attention to His voice and carry out His command. In accordance with Colossians 3:20, children should always obey their parents.

Conclusion

God promises those who honour their parents a life, joy, and security that others will not know. Take note that this applies to the modern church as well as Old Testament Israel. The promise and the edict are both renewed by Paul. But now, instead of being in Canaan, the Promised Land is in the wide-open spaces, atop the high mountains, and alongside the pristine shores of a new land where God now dwells (Revelation 21:1-3). And even now, as we travel through this cursed and crumbling world in Christ, our souls breathe that air and enjoy that pleasure.
